What is Tower of Hanoi?

The Towers of Hanoi, also known as the pyramid puzzle, is a mathematical game involving three rods and disks stacked in descending order, with the objective of moving the entire stack to one of the other rods such that the order remains the same. It is a captivating mathematical puzzle that has intrigued minds for centuries, challenging even the most seasoned problem solvers.

Where did it originate?

Legend has it that the Tower of Hanoi puzzle traces its roots to an ancient temple in the mystical city of Varanasi in India. According to the tale, there exists a temple with three diamond pillars and 64 golden disks. Brahma, the Hindu god of creation, placed these disks in a specific order at the beginning of time. The priests of the temple are tasked with the eternal challenge of moving the disks from one pillar to another, adhering to the rules that no disk may be placed on top of a smaller one. It is believed that once the Tower of Brahma is complete, the world would end. In the 19th century, French mathematician Edouard Lucas formalized the Tower of Hanoi puzzle, presenting it to the world in 1883 through his column, "Récréations Mathématiques," in the newspaper "La Nature." It quickly gained popularity as an engaging and intellectually stimulating problem.

What are the rules of the puzzle?

The objective of the Tower of Hanoi puzzle is to move all the disks from the left rod to the right rod. However, while this seems simple at first, there are a few rules that make this classic puzzle game such a favorite among puzzlers and math teachers alike:

  • Only one disk may be moved at a time
  • You can only move the disk that is on top of a stack
  • Each disk can be placed on any rod, but it cannot be placed on top of a smaller disk

How many moves to complete the puzzle?

Each Tower of Hanoi problem has a minimum number of moves to solve it that is based on the amount of disks. In a 3-disk problem, a minimum of 7 moves are required.So every time the number of disks is increased, that minimum number of moves is doubled + 1. Thus, the minimum number of moves required to solve a Tower of Hanoi is 2N-1. In this case, the “N” represents the amount of starting disks.

Why is it significant?

Solving the Tower of Hanoi problem demonstrates the power and elegance of recursive algorithms in problem-solving.

How do we solve it?

By recursively breaking down the problem into smaller subproblems and solving them, the recursive formula efficiently calculates the minimum number of moves required to solve the Tower of Hanoi problem for any given number of disks. When dealing with any number of disks in the Tower of Hanoi puzzle, the algorithm follows a straightforward sequence of three key steps:

  1. Move (n-1) Disks: Shifting the upper (n-1) disks from the source tower to the auxiliary tower, using the destination tower as a guide.
  2. Move nth Disk: Transferring the largest nth disk from the source tower to the destination tower.
  3. Recursive Moves: Repeating the entire process recursively, focusing on handling (n-1) disks in each iteration, until all disks are successfully moved to the destination tower.
